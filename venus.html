Although Venus has very similar size and interior strecture as Earth, it's valcanic surface and extremely hot and dense atmosphere makes it one of the most inhospitable places in the Solar System.

Atmosphere

Thick clouds of sulfuric acid and dust reflect most of the Sun's light back to space, while carbon dioxide traps the Sun's heat inside the atmosphere, causing a runaway greeenhose effect. The Atmospheric pressure on the surface of Venus is 92 times greater than on Earth.
