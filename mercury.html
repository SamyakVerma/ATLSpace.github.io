Mercury is the innermost and the smallest planet of the Solar System. Because it has almost no atmosphere to retain heat, Mercury's surface experiences the greatest temperature variation of all the planets

OBSERVATION

Mercury appears as a morning or eveing star form Earth, but due to it's proximity to the Sun, it is very hard to see. Still, Mercury can be observed twice a year : in springs at dusk and in autumm before dawn

Structure

The curst in similar is appearance to the that of the Moon with mare-like plains and heavy crateing. A distance feature of the surface crust is the presence of numerous narrow ridges, which might have formed as Mercury's core and mental cooled and contracted after the crust has solified.

CORE

Mercury's extreme high density infers that the planet has large, iron-righ core, with higher iron content than that of any other major planet in the Solar System
